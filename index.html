Multi-Species Interacting Particle System

This project implements a continuous two-dimensional multi-species particle model inspired by cellular automata principles. Although particles evolve in continuous space, the system updates in discrete time steps using deterministic local interaction rules. Complex spatial organization emerges purely from short-range attractive and repulsive forces between species.

Project Overview

  • Multi-species interaction matrix
  • Short-range attraction and repulsion forces
  • Velocity damping and reflective boundary conditions
  • Energy monitoring and stability control
  • Emergent clustering and pattern formation

Motivation

Many physical and biological systems exhibit large-scale organization arising from simple local interactions. Examples include bacterial colony growth, flocking systems, reaction-diffusion chemistry, and segregation dynamics. This project explores how a minimal rule-based interaction matrix can generate non-trivial macroscopic structures without centralized control.

Mathematical Framework

Each particle belongs to a species \( s \in \{1, \dots, N\} \). Interactions are governed by a matrix:

\[ A_{ij} \in [-1,1] \]

where:

  • \( A_{ij} > 0 \) → attraction
  • \( A_{ij} < 0 \) → repulsion

For two particles \( i \) and \( j \) separated by distance \( d_{ij} \), and within interaction radius \( r_s \), the force magnitude is:

\[ F_{ij} = A_{s_i s_j} \left( 1 - \frac{d_{ij}}{r_s} \right) \]

Velocity update equation:

\[ \mathbf{v}_i(t+1) = (1 - \eta)\mathbf{v}_i(t) + \sum_{j \neq i} \mathbf{F}_{ij} + \mathbf{g} \]

Position update:

\[ \mathbf{x}_i(t+1) = \mathbf{x}_i(t) + \Delta t \, \mathbf{v}_i(t+1) \]

where:

  • \( \eta \) is the viscosity (damping factor)
  • \( \Delta t \) is the time scale parameter
  • \( \mathbf{g} \) represents optional gravitational acceleration
  • Reflective boundary conditions are applied at domain edges

System Parameters

  • Number of species
  • Particles per species
  • Interaction radius
  • Viscosity (damping)
  • Time scale
  • Gravity
  • Maximum velocity constraint
  • Glow and trail visualization
  • Energy measurement

Emergent Behaviour

Depending on the interaction matrix configuration, the system exhibits:

  • Species segregation
  • Stable radial clustering
  • Spiral and rotating structures
  • Dynamic oscillatory swarms
  • Metastable collective states
  • Self-assembled particle aggregates

The model demonstrates how deterministic microscopic interaction rules can produce complex macroscopic organization — a fundamental concept in cellular automata, statistical physics, and complex systems theory.

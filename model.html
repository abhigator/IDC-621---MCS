Mathematical Model

The system consists of \( N \) interacting particles in a two-dimensional domain. Each particle \( i \) is characterized by:

  • Position: \( \mathbf{x}_i = (x_i, y_i) \)
  • Velocity: \( \mathbf{v}_i = (v_{x,i}, v_{y,i}) \)
  • Species index: \( s_i \in \{1, \dots, M\} \)
Particles interact only within a finite interaction radius \( R_{s_i} \). ---

Interaction Matrix

Species interactions are defined by a matrix:

\[ A_{ij} \in [-1, 1] \]

where:

  • \( A_{ij} > 0 \) → attraction
  • \( A_{ij} < 0 \) → repulsion
  • \( A_{ij} = 0 \) → no interaction
---

Force Law

For two particles \( i \) and \( j \) separated by distance:

\[ d_{ij} = \| \mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_i \| \]

If \( d_{ij} < R_{s_i} \), the force applied on particle \( i \) is:

\[ \mathbf{F}_{ij} = A_{s_i s_j} \left( 1 - \frac{d_{ij}}{R_{s_i}} \right) \frac{\mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_i}{d_{ij}} \]

The force magnitude decreases linearly with distance and vanishes at \( d_{ij} = R_{s_i} \).

---

Total Force

The net force on particle \( i \) is:

\[ \mathbf{F}_i = \sum_{j \ne i} \mathbf{F}_{ij} \]
---

Time Evolution

Velocity Update

\[ \mathbf{v}_i(t+1) = (1 - \eta)\mathbf{v}_i(t) + \mathbf{F}_i + \mathbf{g} \]

Position Update

\[ \mathbf{x}_i(t+1) = \mathbf{x}_i(t) + \Delta t \, \mathbf{v}_i(t+1) \]
---

Parameters

  • \( \eta \) — viscosity (damping coefficient)
  • \( \Delta t \) — time scale parameter
  • \( \mathbf{g} \) — gravitational acceleration (optional)
  • Reflective boundary conditions applied at domain edges
---

Energy Measurement

The total kinetic energy of the system is computed as:

\[ E(t) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \| \mathbf{v}_i(t) \|^2 \]

Energy serves as a global measure of dynamical activity in the system.
